PNP vs Express Entry Streams

Two steps: province, then IRCC.

PNP applicants receive a provincial nomination, then enter the federal pool (often with an enhanced nomination tied to Express Entry). The clock AORTrack uses for “processing time” is the federal Acknowledgement of Receipt through eCOPR — where PNP files often sit longer due to extra verification and higher variance by province.

Frequently Asked

PNP processing time — common questions.

What is “PNP processing time” on this page?
We mean the federal stage: from your Acknowledgement of Receipt (AOR) after submitting your PR application as a provincial nominee, through to eCOPR. Provincial nomination processing time before that is a separate timeline.
Why is PNP slower than CEC or FSW on the median?
Nominees often need extra verification (employment, nomination conditions, documents from two governments). Variance by province and stream is high — which is why the P25–P75 window is wider than for CEC or FSW in our community data.
Does AORTrack split PNP by province?
Cohort stats group by stream, type, province, and AOR month where the data supports it. The headline figures here pool PNP federal-stage timelines; start tracking to see how your province compares.
Is this official IRCC processing time?
No. All numbers are crowd-sourced from AORTrack users. They are useful for relative comparison and cohort context, not as a government guarantee.
Where can I compare CEC, FSW, and PNP side by side?
Use our processing stats dashboard, or open the CEC and FSW stream pages for the same layout with stream-specific copy.
